## Description:
Scaenatio is obsessed with theatre, treating the lives of mortals as merely three-act structures. Their true form is unknown, although they interface with mortals in five different costumes, each a separate aspect. These aspects do not exist at the same time, but their control over each of their respective parts of Scaenatio's full domain never lapses despite that. People rarely worship all five aspects, usually choosing just the aspects that pertain to them at any given moment. Scaenatio switches between aspects at irregular intervals, and even the least temperamental of their aspects is still capable of extreme cruelty in the name of the show.  

- 5 aspects:
	- Pathos, Aspect of Tragedy (Sadness)
		- Pathos exists in a state of constant misery—or at least that's how they portray themselves to the world. In fact, it seems that they take quiet joy in bringing others down to their level. Often, Pathos finds those who are at their lowest and forces them even lower to watch how it plays out. Or perhaps they'll offer them a modicum of hope to watch it grow and be dashed. However, for those who survive the tragedy and reach an acceptable ending, Pathos rewards greatly.
	- Ethos, Aspect of the Pact (Anger)
		- When someone is designated a role, it is their obligation to fulfill that role to their utmost ability. Ethos, as such, is very strict on those who break such promises. They are the one who upholds magical pacts, contracts, and deals. They are the one who brings karma down upon oathbreakers and liars. And they are the one who controls the power of names. What is a name but a casting in a role, after all?
	- Logos, Aspect of Continuum (Fear)
		- The show must go on, steadily, constantly, unshakably on and on. Time is the foundation of this clown show that we run, and someone must maintain it. That is only logical, and Logos is nothing but. Nothing but. On and on. Until the universe is done. 
	- Kairos, Aspect of the Backdrop (Calm)
		- Everyone is so focused on the characters of life; no one stops to pay attention to the background. And that is exactly Kairos' role: to tell the rain to rain and the storms to storm. Although the truly disastrous forms of weather are better left to the Ard'okaal, Kairos is in the business of the quainter world of weather where the beauty of the theatre lies. Don't waste time trying to find Kairos, though. Their wanderlust knows no bounds. 
	- Bathos, Aspect of the Fae (Joy)
		- For someone of such immense divine power, one would expect them to take their role much more seriously. However, for Bathos, the Faerie Queen, reality is much too trite to enjoy it dry. No, existence is much more palatable with a little bit of comedy. The Fae live in a world of whimsy and joy, always searching for a laugh, and those who support Bathos—often comedians, actors, or mages studying Fae transmutation magic—are welcome to worship Bathos. Just pray that your life doesn't become the punchline of the joke.
